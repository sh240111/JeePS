How to Survive Jeepney Commuting During Heavy Rain in Zamboanga City (Student Edition)

Being a commuter student when heavy rain falls in Zamboanga City leads to one word: struggle. When streets drown in floods, jeepneys get stuck in traffic, uniforms get wet, and the constant fear of getting your bag soaked comes one after the other, it’s natural that we may fall into panic. But, we also have no choice but to keep moving forward because there’s more to life than just our assignments and performance tasks waiting for us at home.

When it’s raining, commuting doesn’t stop. So, here’s how I’ve learned to be prepared and what to actually do when the rain gets bad.

When Leaving the House, Be Prepared

Always bring a small umbrella

Always bring an umbrella with you no matter how “okay” the sky looks. Zamboanga City’s weather transitions fast—within minutes, a small sprinkle can turn into a heavy rain shower. Having a compact umbrella inside your bag can really help in not getting you drenched while waiting for a jeepney or walking to your destination.

Waterproof your bag as much as you can

A waterproof bag is a necessity. But if you don’t have one, it’s better to improvise using plastic folders, ziplock bags, or even extra plastic inside your bag to protect notebooks, gadgets, and important papers. Always assume water will get inside the jeepney.

Wear shoes you can sacrifice

Heavy rain often means flooded roads—which usually means wet shoes. Avoid wearing your favorite or most delicate shoes so as not to damage them. It’s best to wear shoes that dry fast or bring slippers to change into when you arrive.

Pack extra clothes or at least an extra shirt

This sounds dramatic until you are actually caught in the situation. Even just an extra shirt can make a big difference if your uniform gets soaked on the way to school.

When Caught in Heavy Rain during the Commute

Choose your seat wisely

Try to sit farther from the open sides of the jeepney. Rain tends to splash in from the sides, especially when other vehicles pass by. Sitting closer to the center helps keep you drier.

Keep your bag up and close to you

Place your bag on your lap instead of on the floor. Jeepney floors collect water fast, and once your bag touches it, there’s no going back. Holding it higher protects your things.

Don’t rush getting on or off

Slippery steps and muddy roads are accidents waiting to happen. Take your time when boarding or getting off. Slowly but surely.

Be patient with the delay

Heavy rain means traffic, flooding, and slow movement. You can’t change the situation, but you can change how you react by adjusting expectations. Everything will take longer—and that’s okay.

After Getting Off, Control the Damage

Dry yourself as soon as possible

Wipe your shoes, bag, and arms once you reach shelter. The faster you dry your things, the less uncomfortable the rest of your day will be.

Change clothes if you can

Sitting in wet clothes for hours is uncomfortable and unhealthy. If you brought extra clothes, use them. If not, at least dry off as much as possible.

Check your belongings immediately

Make sure your notes, books, and gadgets are safe. Catching moisture early can prevent bigger damage later.

Heavy rain in Zamboanga City is inevitable, especially during its season. But being a prepared jeepney commuter student makes a huge difference. You may still get wet, tired, or delayed—but at least you won’t be completely unprepared.

In a place where rain can interfere with everything, learning how to move through it becomes a skill every commuter eventually masters.
